Instruction Sets for Stranger at Flatiron Building

Instruction Sets for Stranger at Flatiron Building was the first project of the semester. With a partner we were tasked to setup an installation at any place in New York attempting to get stangers to interact with it or amongst themselves.This project was completed in 4 parts starting with

Part 1: research, observation, and documentation
Determine a space away from the New School campus and research its history. Observe the space and use a variety of visual strategies to map its use over time; What kinds of patterns arise from your observations? What kinds of narrative does the space suggest?

Part 2: prototype 1
Propose a designed intervention of any type, scale, or media that is specific for that space. Create an instruction set or interface that does not employ spoken or written language. Think about what you can realistically prototype and iterate in your chosen space and given time frame. Communicate your results in group presentation and critique.

Part 3: prototype 2
Observe and document your initial prototype. Considering ideas of invitation and the nature of the intended experience, iterate and document. Think about the qualities you would like your work to have, the nature of the core experience
